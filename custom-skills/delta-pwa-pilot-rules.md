# Delta PWA: Pilot Rules, Duties & Rotations

How the Delta Pilot Working Agreement (PWA, with ALPA) and FAR 117 generate pilot
duties, pairings/rotations, lines, and reserve. The PWA is the governing contract;
FAR 117 is the federal floor beneath it. Section numbers below are orientation
aids — confirm against the current PWA and the Delta Scheduling/PBS Reference
Handbooks, which Delta and the MEC publish and update.

## 1. Structure: categories, bases, seats, fleets

A pilot holds a **category** = base (domicile) × fleet × seat. Seat is Captain
(CA) or First Officer (FO); fleet is the aircraft type (e.g. A220, 717, 737, A321,
757/767 "7ER", A330, A350). Bases include ATL (the largest), plus DTW, MSP, NYC,
SLC, LAX, SEA, BOS, CVG, and others. Bidding, seniority, lines, and pay rates are
all category-specific (e.g. "ATL73NB CA" = Atlanta 737 narrowbody Captain). Pilots
move categories by bidding; a category change triggers training and re-bidding.

Seniority (date-of-hire based) governs nearly everything: category/seat bidding,
schedule bidding, and the order in which PBS awards trips and days off.

## 2. The building blocks: duty period, pairing/rotation, line

- **Duty period** — a single work day from report to release; may contain one or
  more flight segments (legs). Bounded by FAR 117 FDP limits and PWA duty rules.
- **Pairing (rotation / trip)** — a sequence of one or more duty periods that
  starts and ends at the pilot's base, with layovers (rest) between duty periods.
  Delta calls it a *rotation*; "a grouping of flights assigned together under a
  single identifier." Length is typically 1–4+ days. The pairing is the atomic unit
  of crew scheduling.
- **Line (schedule)** — a pilot's monthly assignment, built from pairings (for
  lineholders) or on-call days (for reserves). Monthly value is measured in **credit
  hours**.

## 3. Pay vs. credit vs. block — rigs and guarantees

Three different clocks; keeping them distinct is the heart of crew analytics:

- **Block** — actual flight time, brake-release to brake-set (block-to-block).
- **Duty** — time on the clock within a duty period (report to release).
- **Credit (pay credit)** — the hours actually paid, which is the *greater* of
  block and a set of contractual minimums:
  - **Minimum day / duty-period guarantee** — a floor of credit per duty period.
  - **Duty rig** — credit as a function of duty time (e.g. a ratio guaranteeing a
    minimum credit for each hour of duty), so long, low-block days still pay fairly.
  - **Trip / TAFB rig** — credit as a function of total Time Away From Base, so
    multi-day trips with long layovers earn a minimum per elapsed time.
  - **Monthly guarantees** — a lineholder's regular **line guarantee** (a floor of
    monthly credit; Delta's is 65:00) and the reserve guarantee (below).
  The pilot is paid the maximum of these mechanisms. The ratio of credit to block
  ("pay-above-block") is the key productivity/cost metric — rigs and guarantees
  raise it.
- **ALV (Average Line Value)** — the target monthly credit around which lines are
  built for a category, set within contractual bounds each bid period (e.g.
  narrowbody/7ER lines roughly 72:00–84:00; other widebody roughly 71:00–85:00).

Note: exact rig ratios and minimum-day values live in the PWA pay/scheduling
sections — describe them structurally and confirm the numbers; don't assume.

## 4. PBS line construction

Delta builds monthly lines with a **Preferential Bidding System (PBS)** (NAVBLUE
WebApp). Pilots submit prioritized **bid preferences** (trips, days off, report
times, layovers, credit window, avoidances); PBS awards in seniority order,
constructing each pilot a line that honors as many preferences as possible while
satisfying coverage and contractual line-construction parameters. The monthly bid
package (released by ~1800E on the 4th) contains the rotations available for the
category, line-construction parameters, and reserve rules. Outcomes:

- **Regular lineholder** — awarded a line of pairings near the ALV, guaranteed at
  least the regular line guarantee (65:00).
- **Reserve line** — awarded on-call availability (see §5) when seniority can't hold
  a regular line.
- **Reduced/recovery line types** (e.g. RLL) — special lines for pilots PBS can't
  build a regular line for; a mix of awarded trips, open-time pickups, and recovery
  days/assignments.

PBS reference handbooks document mechanics, but the PWA and FARs supersede them
where they differ.

## 5. Reserve

Reserve pilots are paid a **reserve guarantee** (based on the category ALV) to be
available on **on-call days**; days off on a reserve line are **X-days** (with
some **golden X-days** that are higher-value/protected), and the number granted
depends on reserve guarantee, staffing, and reserve days in the bid period. Reserve
flavors include short-call vs. long-call availability and specialized types (e.g.
ULC). Reserve pilots are assigned trips from open time under reserve-assignment
rules (distinct from recovery rules). Reserve **availability vs. required** counts
(Section 23 W) drive whether drops/swaps can be granted on a given day — i.e.
reserve depth gates the whole operation's flexibility.

## 6. Schedule changes: drops, swaps, premium open time

After lines are awarded, pilots adjust schedules through:

- **PCS (Pilot Change Schedule)** — the system for dropping/adding/moving trips,
  processed at set daily times, subject to reserve availability.
- **Personal Drop (PD) / Authorized Personal Drop (APD)** — drop trips or on-call
  days when reserve coverage allows (APD has less restrictive reserve requirements
  than PD); dropped credit reduces the line/reserve guarantee accordingly.
- **Pilot-to-Pilot Swap Board** — advertise/trade trips directly with other pilots.
- **Premium open time** — picking up open flying at premium rates via "slips":
  green slips (GS), white slips (WS), yellow slips (YS), super slips (SS), and
  "swap with the pot." These cover flying that opened up or that senior pilots
  dropped, at higher pay — a key coverage tool and a cost/understaffing signal.

## 7. Recovery, reroute, and assignment

When the operation breaks, the company can **reroute** a pilot (change their trip
in progress), **assign** open flying (including inverse/junior assignment of the
most-junior available pilot), or use **recovery flying** on recovery-line days
(rules differing from reserve). These assignments carry their own pay/guarantee
protections (e.g. rotation guarantee when a future trip is removed due to overfly,
reroute, or assignment). The distinction between *reserve* assignment and
*recovery* assignment matters for both legality and pay.

## 8. FAR 117: the federal floor

For Part 121 passenger pilots, FAR 117 sets fatigue limits the PWA must meet or
beat:

- **Flight Duty Period (FDP)** maximums by report time and number of segments
  (longer when starting at favorable times / fewer legs; shorter for early/late or
  many-leg days).
- **Flight time** limits within an FDP (8–9 hours), plus cumulative caps (e.g. 100
  flight hours in 28 days; 1,000 in 365 days).
- **Rest** — a minimum consecutive rest before an FDP (10 hours with an 8-hour
  sleep opportunity), and a weekly rest (≥30 consecutive hours in 168).
- **Augmentation** — adding a 3rd/4th pilot with onboard rest facilities (Class
  1/2/3) extends FDP for long-haul; this shapes widebody pairing construction.
- **Reserve rest** rules for long-call/short-call.

The PWA frequently provides more protective limits; when both apply, the more
restrictive (crew-protective) governs. Confirm current 117 specifics against the
regulation.

## 9. What to verify

Treat as confirm-before-use: exact rig ratios and minimum-day values, current
guarantee and ALV numbers, the precise list of bases/fleets, reserve X-day tables,
slip pay rates, and all section numbers. Authoritative sources: the current PWA,
the Delta MEC **Scheduling Reference Handbook** and **PBS Reference Handbook**, and
the FARs. These are updated periodically.
